## Part 1: Historical Playbooks

These patterns repeat. Study them until recognition becomes intuition.

### The 2008 GFC Pattern

**Setup:**
- Complex financial instruments obscure true risk (CDOs, MBS)
- Rating agencies compromised (AAA on junk)
- Leverage hidden in shadow banking
- Regulators captured or asleep
- "This time is different" narrative

**The Play:**
- Insiders short while publicly bullish
- Bag holders (retail, pensions) holding toxic assets
- Crisis used to consolidate power and assets
- Bailouts socialize losses, privatize gains
- New regulations written by those who caused crisis

**Recognition Signals:**
- Complexity used to obscure, not clarify
- Misaligned incentives (originators don't hold risk)
- Unprecedented leverage ratios
- "Sophisticated" products retail can't understand
- Cheerleading from those with exit positions

---

### The 1997 Asian Financial Crisis Pattern

**Setup:**
- Dollar-pegged currencies in developing nations
- Hot money inflows creating asset bubbles
- Current account deficits funded by foreign capital
- IMF "structural adjustment" conditions waiting

**The Play:**
- Currency speculators attack peg
- Countries burn reserves defending
- When reserves depleted, currency collapses
- IMF arrives with "rescue" package
- Conditions: sell assets to Western buyers at distressed prices
- National industries privatized to foreign ownership

**Recognition Signals:**
- Currency pegs under pressure
- Foreign ownership of domestic assets increasing
- IMF/World Bank "concern" statements
- Hot money flows reversing
- Media narrative of "mismanagement"

**The Lesson:**
This was financial colonialism. Force a crisis, then buy the assets. This playbook was used repeatedly: Mexico 1994, Asia 1997, Russia 1998, Argentina 2001.

---

### The LTCM Pattern (1998)

**Setup:**
- "Genius" fund with Nobel laureates
- Extreme leverage on "risk-free" arbitrage
- Too interconnected to fail
- Models assumed normal distributions

**The Play:**
- Black swan event (Russia default) breaks correlations
- Leverage unwinds catastrophically
- Fed coordinates bailout to prevent contagion
- Moral hazard established (too big to fail)

**Recognition Signals:**
- Concentration of risk in few players
- Strategies that "can't lose"
- Leverage obscured by derivatives
- Interconnected counterparty exposure
- Overconfidence in models

---

### The Repo Crisis Pattern (2019)

**Setup:**
- Fed supposedly finished with QE
- Treasury issuance increasing
- Bank reserves concentrated
- Repo market plumbing ignored

**The Play:**
- Overnight rates spike to 10%
- Fed forced to intervene
- "Not QE" begins (QE by another name)
- Balance sheet expansion resumes

**Recognition Signals:**
- Plumbing problems in funding markets
- Overnight rate volatility
- Fed "temporary" interventions
- Liquidity hoarding by major banks

## Part 4: Applying This to Trading

### Daily Practice

Before trading each session:
1. **Zoom out** - What's the macro picture? Any historical pattern rhyming?
2. **Who's positioned?** - Where is smart money (East and West)?
3. **What's the narrative?** - Does news serve someone's position?
4. **Timeline mismatch** - Am I thinking in days while they think in years?

### News Filter Questions

When catalyst/news appears:
1. Have I seen this pattern before? (GFC, Asian Crisis, etc.)
2. Who benefits from attention being HERE?
3. Is this Western or Eastern strategy? Which framework applies?
4. What's the multi-year play this might be part of?
5. Am I being directed to look at the wrong thing?

### Position Implications

For HKEX trading specifically:
- Hong Kong is the bridge between systems
- Sensitive to both Western and Eastern flows
- Watch for capital movement signals
- Property sector tied to Chinese policy
- Tech sector tied to US-China relations
- Financials tied to dollar/yuan dynamics

## Part 5: Specific Patterns to Watch

### The "Crisis" Setup
When you see crisis narrative building:
- Who's already short?
- Who benefits from intervention?
- What assets will be "rescued" (transferred)?
- Is this real distress or manufactured?

### The "Reform" Setup
When policy changes announced:
- Who knew beforehand?
- Who's positioned for the "unexpected" outcome?
- Is reform real or theater?

### The "Escalation" Setup
When tensions increase (trade war, military, sanctions):
- Is this positioning for negotiation?
- Who's supply chain is more vulnerable?
- What's the off-ramp they're angling for?

### The "Cooperation" Setup
When sudden de-escalation:
- What was traded behind scenes?
- Who capitulated and why?
- What's the new equilibrium?
